It took me a few days to figure out what I should make, since there was a lot of choices available. What gave me a rough idea of what to make was this image that the teacher showed as an example(might be different, since i took this image off the internet):

After that, I thought to myself: "What if I made it such that you could move something in this model, but never be able to take it out no matter how you try?" Then I started drawing a simple idea in autocad

I then started making a model in fusion360.

So firstly, I made a box. Then I shelled it with 2mm wall thickness. Following that, I made a hole on 4 sides, each a different shape. Afterwards, I extruded the shape matching the holes. Lastly, I extruded an extension at the back and extruded a smaller cube in the middle to prevent it from being taken out from either side.

When I sliced the model, I realized I needed support, and the printing time was 48mins, so I scaled it in the Cura app to 20mmx20mmx16.7mm, which reduced the time taken to 20mins.
